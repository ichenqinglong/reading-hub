The Smoothie Stand Bluff

一个假装开除的把戏，让愤怒的顾客自己道了歉

Full Article Audio Listen to the complete story

✦ Target Vocabulary

  • tactics策略，手段
  • intimidating令人畏惧的
  • capitulate屈服，投降
  • tact圆滑，得体
01

Taylor was seventeen and working weekends at a mall smoothie stand when she learned that the best way to handle an angry customer was not to argue back.

02

Her shift lead, Ethan, had worked out the tactics first. One Saturday, a man in a polo shirt started yelling because his drink was not sweet enough. Before Taylor could respond, Ethan stepped in, stone-faced. "Taylor, hand over your badge. You are suspended for the rest of the day — and I am docking your pay."

03

Taylor played along, eyes wide. "Please, Ethan. I need that money for my phone bill."

04

The man went quiet. What had been intimidating thirty seconds ago — a grown adult, red-faced, leaning over the counter — suddenly looked small. He capitulated completely, apologizing twice before he left.

05

Ethan had handled the whole thing with tact: no argument, no raised voice, just a mirror held up to the customer's own behavior. They have used the trick a dozen times since. It works almost every time — and watching someone realize their words could actually cost a teenager their pay is, Taylor says, deeply satisfying.

Comprehension

Question 01

What did Ethan do when the customer started yelling at Taylor?

Ethan stepped in with a stone-faced expression and pretended to suspend Taylor, asking her to hand over her badge and telling her he was docking her pay. It was a performance — a tactic to make the angry customer realize how his behavior was affecting a real person.

Question 02

In the story, "capitulated" most likely means...

  • Abecame angrier and louder

Question 03

为什么这个假开除的把戏几乎每次都有效？顾客的心理是什么？

这个把戏之所以有效，是因为它像一面镜子（a mirror held up to the customer's own behavior）。当顾客看到自己的愤怒似乎真的导致一个十几岁的孩子被处罚、失去收入时，他们内心的愧疚感会立刻被激活。他们意识到自己正在对一个比自己弱势得多的人施压，这种认知让"intimidating"的行为瞬间变得不可持续，于是选择 capitulate — 让步道歉。
